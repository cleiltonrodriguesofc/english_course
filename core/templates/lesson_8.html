Ordinal Numbers 🥇🥈🥉

We use ordinal numbers to talk about dates, positions, and order.

💡 General Rule: Just add -TH to the number (four → fourth, six → sixth).
⚠️ Exceptions: 1st (first), 2nd (second), 3rd (third), 5th (fifth), 8th (eighth), 9th (ninth), 12th (twelfth).

How to Say Dates 🗓️

⚠️ Watch out! English formats are tricky!
01/02/2026 in USA = January 2nd, 2026 (Month / Day / Year)
01/02/2026 in UK = February 1st, 2026 (Day / Month / Year)
Format Written Spoken (listen 🔊)
🇺🇸 American (EUA) March 2, 2026 March second, twenty twenty-six 🔊
🇬🇧 British (UK) 2 March 2026 The second of March, twenty twenty-six 🔊

Telling the Time ⏰

We usually divide the clock in "past" and "to".

07:00
Seven o'clock
São sete horas.
07:05
Five past seven
São sete e cinco. (5 minutos passados das 7)
07:15
Quarter past seven
São sete e quinze. (um quarto de hora passado)
07:30
Half past seven
São sete e meia. (meia hora passado)

AM vs PM

AM: Midnight to noon (00:00 - 11:59)
PM: Noon to midnight (12:00 - 23:59)
